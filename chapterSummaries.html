Chapter Summaries

  • Chapter 1
  • The novel begins by the narrator introducing himself and the setting, which is December 2001
  • The narrator then shifts into telling of an event that occurred in 1975 while he was a twelve year old boy living in Afghanistan
  • The narrator does not yet give insight into the event other than by explaining that the event changed who he is forever
  • He then goes into telling about a phone call he received in the summer of 2000 by a friend living in Pakistan, Rahim Khan
  • Khan asks Amir, the narrator, to come visit him
  • Following the phone call Amir goes on a walk through San Francisco, his new hometown and contemplates the conversation with Khan
  • While on the walk he notices people flying kites and he thinks back to his friend Hassan from when he lived in Afghanistan, he describes Hassan as a young boy with a cleft lip that he remembers as a kite runner
  • Chapter 2
  • Amir recalls back to when he and Hassan were children and would constantly cause mischief
  • They would often climb trees and use mirrors to reflect sunlight into neighbors windows or sometimes shoot walnuts at neighbor dogs with slingshots
  • Amir admits that these were however all his ideas yet Hassan never was upset with Amir if they were caught
  • Amir lived in an upscale house in Kabul with his father, Baba, however Hassan was not quite as fortunate as he lived with his father, Ali, in a small hut on Baba's estate as Ali was a servant to Baba
  • Both of the boys' mothers are no longer around as Amir's died while she was giving birth and Hassan's disappeared when he was very young
  • The boys are taking a walk one day when an Afghan soldier harasses Hassan by saying that he slept with Hassan's mother
  • Sanaubar, Hassan's mother, and Ali were quite an odd match when they were seeing one another
  • Ali was significantly older than her, and he also was disfigured from the effects of polio, meanwhile Sanaubar was very beautiful and immoral
  • It was a common belief that the marriage was only arranged by Sanaubar's father to restore good standing in the community, and further support came to this when Sanaubar ran away only five days after giving birth
  • The soldier further insults Hassan by calling him a Hazara, an insult, as the Hazara are a persecuted ethnic group during this time and both of Hassan's parents were Hazara
  • Amir on the other hand comes from a non-persecuted ethnic group known as the Pashtun
  • Hassan later learns more about the reason why the Hazara are persecuted from a history book
  • He learns that the Hazara tried to start an uprising during the 1800's however it was put down by the Pashtuns
  • The Hazara are also referred to by many racist and derogatory terms as the Pashtuns further do not like them because the Hazara are Shia Muslim while the Pashtuns are Sunni Muslims
  • Chapter 3
  • Amir begins to tell the readers some details about his father describing him as a large man, about six foot five, with curly dark hair, and a thick beard
  • Amir adds that he even wrestled a bear once before going into detail on his successes
  • Many times Baba was doubted, yet he always overcame his challenges
  • He was never trained as an architect yet built a successful orphanage, he was not considered a savvy business man yet he became one of the most successful ones in Kabul, and despite his low social class, he married Sofia Akrami, a beautiful woman who hailed from a powerful bloodline
  • Baba also was a very wise men with a strong personal moral compass, Amir provides an example of one time when Baba was pouring himself a glass of liquor Amir remarked that his teacher, Mullah Fatiullah Khan, had said that drinking was a sin
  • Baba responds by saying that the only sin was theft and all other sins are related to theft, such as murder, which is the theft of ones life, Baba says that men like Mullah are dumb
  • Amir spends much of his young life trying to earn his fathers respect by pursuing his fathers interests, however Amir is personally more interested in such things as poetry and reading, yet he fears his father does not view these as manly endeavors
  • Amir tells a story about a sporting event the two attend, it is a game called buzkashi which involves horse riders trying to put an animal carcass in a scoring circle
  • During the event one rider is trampled to death causing Amir to cry, meanwhile Baba looks down on him with disdain
  • Following the event Amir hears Baba on the phone with Rahim Khan, the man from the first chapter, discussing Amir
  • Baba remarks that Amir is weak and cannot stand up for himself, he adds that he is afraid that as an adult he will be unable to do so either
  • Chapter 4
  • The story shifts back to Baba's childhood, 1933 in particular
  • It is the year that Baba is born, meanwhile the King of Afghanistan is Zahir Shah
  • At the time Baba is just one year old
  • Ali, Hassan's father, is also very young at the time as well, and it is in 1933 when both of his parents are killed by a drunk driver leaving Ali all alone in the world
  • Baba's father steps in and rescues the kid by adopting him and raising him as his own
  • Despite growing up together, Baba would look back and say that him and Ali were never friends probably because of their religious and ethnic differences
  • Not ironically when Amir looks at his and Hassan's relationship as kids, he does not view him as a friend for the same reasons their fathers were not really friends
  • Despite the fact that they played together often they led very different lives, Amir would attend school during the days, while the illiterate Hassan would do chores around the estate
  • Often times Amir would read to Hassan, the two shared a favorite story, "Rostam and Sohrab" which tells the story of two men Rostam and Sohrab, Rostam wounds the other fatally in battle and later learns that he actually killed his own son
  • One day while Amir is reading to Hassan he decides to stray from the book and tell his own story, which surprisingly Hassan claims is the best story he has ever heard
  • Encouraged, Amir decides to begin writing his own short stories
  • His first story is about a man who cries pearls instead of tears, when he learns his amazing talent he begins to make himself sad on purpose in order to become rich
  • In the end his talent leads to great suffering as he sits upon a pile of pearls and mourns the death of his wife who he stabbed to induce sadness
  • Amir is proud of his story and tries to show his father, however Baba does not pay much attention to him as he is currently in a conversation with Rahim Kahn
  • Amir is upset at first, however later that evening he receives a note from his father saying that he has a great talent
  • Amir is so happy by this he rushes to find Hassan and wakes him to read him the story
  • Hassan believes the story is wonderful, however he comes up with an idea which annoys and frustrates Amir
  • Hassan says that the man could have just made himself cry with onions
  • Amir is so frustrated that he has a terrible thought about Hassan being a Hazara, yet he holds it in to himself
  • Chapter 5
  • One evening gunfire shakes the streets nearby Amir's home, he along with Hassan and Ali hide for the evening
  • Amir explains that this night marked the beginning of a major change to the Afghanistan they once knew
  • Over the next few years Afghanistan would completely change, first with the communist take over in 1978, then further more with the Soviet invasion in 1979
  • The gunshots that night turn out to be that of a coup in which a cousin of the current king took over and instilled a republic government
  • The cousin is Daoud Khan, and what he does will forever haunt the country
  • The shots had caused the streets to be shut down, which prevents Baba from returning home till the next morning
  • The two boys hear their fathers discussing what has happened, yet they do not understand what it means
  • They decide to go climb trees that day, and while they are walking Hassan is hit by a rock thrown by Assef, a neighborhood bully
  • Assef and two other boys then harass Hassan and criticize him and his dad, they go so far as to call him flat-nose, which is considered a great insult
  • Assef then asks if the boys know about the new republic, he then gives some insight into what it means
  • Assef explains that his father is good friends with Daoud Khan, and that Khan has ideas about purifying Afghanistan for the Pashtuns by eliminating the Hazaras much like Hitler tried with the Aryans and Jews
  • Assef then launches at Amir for being friends with Hassan, Assef readies to strike Amir with brass-knuckles before realizing that Hassan has a slingshot aimed at his head
  • This allows Amir and Hassan to escape
  • Shortly after the coup life goes back to normal for the time being
  • When Hassan's birthday arrives he is surprised by a gift from his father and Baba
  • The two introduce him to Dr. Kumar, a plastic surgeon
  • Dr. Kumar manages to fix Hassan's cleft lip leaving Hassan extremely happy
  • Chapter 6
  • It is winter in Kabul, the best time of year for young boys
  • This is because school is out and the boys can spend time flying kites
  • Amir and Hassan buy kites from a blind man who sells the best kites around
  • The two prepare for the kite fighting tournament that occurs every winter
  • Kite fighting involves two boys going against each other with their kites, the strings are covered in glass, and when one snaps that kite loses
  • The losing kite will then float through the air, and kite runners try to track it down and catch it before it lands
  • The last kite to be cut free is the winner, and Hassan is extremely likely to catch it as he is the best kite runner around
  • Chapter 7
  • In the winter of 1975 the tournament has been decided to be held in Amir's neighborhood, and unlike usual, the other neighborhoods will compete in it as well
  • Before the tournament Baba tells Amir that he might be able to win, Amir sees this as an opportunity to earn his fathers approval and is very excited for the tournament
  • When the tournament begins Amir does very well making it to the last of two kites before winning
  • Amir celebrates with Hassan while his father watches on and gestures for the boys to separate, as it is not good to be seen celebrating with a Hazara
  • Hassan rushes off to track down the losing kite
  • Later that day Amir cannot find Hassan and tries to track him down, he comes across him in an alley way and notices that Assef and two of his friends, Kamal and Wali, are harassing Hassan
  • They try to take the kite from him and berate him for being a Hazara
  • They then say that Amir is not his friend and only keeps him around as a pet
  • Hassan doesn't believe this and defends his friend, however Amir does not stand up for him and stands idly by watching Hassan get beat up
  • As Amir watches from a distance he thinks back to moments during his childhood, the first being that him and Hassan were both breastfed by the same Hazara woman, he then thinks about a time when the two went to see a fortune teller, yet the fortune teller refused to do it for Hassan
  • Amir is then brought back to the present and sees something horrible, two of the boys have pinned down Hassan while Assef takes down his pants and rapes him
  • Amir cannot bring himself to stop it out of fear, he decides to leave and waits till Hassan returns from the alley, Amir pretends that he had been looking for him
  • The two return home without speaking, when Amir finds his father all he can do is burry his head in Baba's chest and cry
  • Chapter 8
  • Following the rape in the alleyway Hassan and Amir spend very little time together
  • Baba and Amir take a trip to Jalalabad to visit Baba's cousin
  • One evening while they are having a traditional Afghan dinner Baba tells of Amir's kite tournament victory, however Amir is extremely bothered by the story due to recollections of what happened after
  • That evening Amir sleeps in the same room along with the rest of the family, however he cannot sleep
  • He remarks out loud that Hassan was raped, however no one is awake to hear it
  • Amir explains that after witnessing the rape he has been an insomniac ever since
  • When Baba and Amir return home, Amir decides to play with Hassan for the first time in a while, however shortly after they meet up and go for a walk Amir changes his mind and decides to return home
  • Amir then begins to completely ignore Hassan and when Hassan questions this Amir tells him to leave him alone
  • The two boys avoid each other from then on out, and Amir even asks Baba if he will get new servants
  • One day Hassan and Amir go again for a walk as Amir promises to read to him, however when they sit beneath a pomegranate tree Amir does not read but instead asks Hassan what he would do if he started throwing pomegranates at him
  • Before Hassan responds, Amir begins to pelt him with the fruit, then he demands Hassan fight back, however Hassan will not
  • Hassan leaves and the two avoid each other again
  • When Amir turns thirteen Baba holds a large party and invites over 400 people
  • At the party Baba makes Amir greet every guest that arrives
  • Assef arrives at the party and when Baba makes Amir greet him, it is quite obvious Amir is unhappy about this
  • Amir refuses to open Assef's gift until he is alone and when he does he finds a biography of Hitler which he immediately throws away
  • While he sits alone Rahim Kahn comes up to join him
  • Rahim explains that when he was younger he nearly married a Hazara woman, yet when his father learned of this he sent the woman and her family away
  • Rahim adds that looking back he is glad he did not marry her as it would have caused a himself and his family shame
  • Rahim adds that he is always available for help before the two return to the party to see the fireworks
  • Back at the party Amir notices Hassan serving Assef and his friends drinks
  • Chapter 9
  • The day after the party Amir takes his time in opening the presents he received at the party
  • As he is leaving home that afternoon Baba stops him to give Amir his present, it is a copy of "Shahnamah", the book that Amir would read to Hassan
  • Amir has made up his mind that Hassan must leave his house so he plans to set him up by taking the money and watch he received for his birthday and putting it under Hassan's pillow before telling his father it was stolen
  • Surprisingly Hassan admits to stealing it and Amir then realizes that Hassan must have saw him watching in the alley way when he was raped
  • Baba forgives Hassan but Ali says that he and Hassan must move away
  • Baba pleads against it but it does not stop them, they leave on a rainy day and Amir refuses to walk them outside or say goodbye
  • Chapter 10
  • It is now 1981 and Amir and Baba are fleeing Afghanistan to Pakistan along with other Afghans
  • They ride in the back of a truck causing Amir to feel sick, however he is afraid that his being sick will embarrass Baba
  • They chose to flee because no one can be trusted anymore and the rafiqs, have taken over the country and divided the society
  • The man driving the truck had apparently had an arrangement to make it pass the Russian checkpoints however at one they are stopped
  • A Russian soldier tells the people in the truck that it will cost one of the woman aboard thirty minutes alone with the soldier in order for them to continue on
  • Baba stands up for her and says he will not allow it
  • The soldier threatens to shoot him, however another soldier steps in and waves them along
  • They eventually reach Jalalabad, however the next truck which is supposed to move them to Pakistan is broken and they must hide with other refugees for a whole week
  • While in hiding Amir notices a boy that he recognized from back home, Kamal
  • Kamal looks very week and does not talk much however, Amir overhears what has happened to him from Kamal's father when he speaks with Baba
  • Before they left for Pakistan Kamal was caught outside by four men who raped him, and ever since then he doesn't speak or interact with anyone
  • They eventually find a truck to transport them on, however it is a fuel truck and the fumes are very tough on the passengers
  • When they arrive in Pakistan Kamal's father notices that Kamal has stopped breathing, enraged he attacks the driver and steals his gun, however instead of shooting the driver he kills himself
  • Chapter 11
  • The novel then shifts to Fremont, California where Baba and Amir have lived for two years
  • Baba still hasn't adjusted to life in America, and Amir tells of one experience when Baba was in a convenience store
  • Baba was asked to present I.D. when he paid with a check, this upset him to the point that he nocks over a magazine rack, in Afghanistan everyone trusted each other to pay properly
  • Amir suggests that night that they return to Pakistan where they stayed for six months before moving to America, however Baba shoots it down
  • Baba insists that they stay so Amir can finish high school and attend college
  • On the night that Amir graduates Baba presents him with a Ford Grand Torino as well as takes him out for a big meal and many drinks
  • That night Amir tells Baba that he would like to study writing in college however Baba rejects this idea as he believes it will be useless
  • Once he receives the car Amir often takes long drives and tries to forget his past
  • Later in 1984 Baba buys an old black van, Amir and him take it garage sale shopping before driving down to a flea market where they sell what they buy for profit
  • One day at the flea market Baba speaks with a man known as General Taheri, Baba tells him that Amir will one day be a great writer
  • While they are talking Taheri's daughter comes over and Amir and her make eye contact
  • Later on the drive home Amir asks Baba about her and learns that she was once romantically involved with a man but it ended badly, Amir cannot take her mind off her after that
  • Chapter 12
  • Amir spends the next year longing for Soraya, General Taheri's daughter, before he builds up the courage to talk to her
  • He makes sure to avoid talking to her when Taheri is around however, until one day when the two are speaking and Taheri walks up
  • Taheri asks Amir to leave and walks him away reminding Amir that he is among other Afghans
  • Amir is upset at first but however quickly pushes it aside when he realizes that Baba has become sick
  • After going to the doctor they learn that Baba has lung cancer however he refuses any treatment
  • Amir doesn't know what to do or who to tell, but Baba forbids him from telling anyone
  • Eventually Baba becomes very sick and must stay in the hospital where he is visited by Afghans nearly every day
  • Amir asks Baba to go to General Taheri and ask for Soraya's hand in marriage for Amir
  • Baba is thrilled by this and does it the next day
  • Taheri accepts and Soraya is happy with the future marriage, however she feels she must explain her past to Amir
  • She tells him that she ran away with an Afghan man when she was 18, and they lived together for a month before Taheri found her and took her home
  • While Soraya was home, Jamila, her love, had a stroke and died
  • Amir is a little perturbed by this but still wants to get married
  • Chapter 13
  • The day following the arrangement, Amir and Baba attend a celebratory dinner at the Taheris' honoring the engagement
  • They plan to do the wedding quickly because of Baba's health, however they want to do it traditionally by the Afghan customs
  • Baba spends nearly 35,000 dollars preparing the wedding ceremony, buying the ring, a tuxedo for Amir, and any other necessities
  • When looking back on the wedding Amir remembers sitting on a couch with his new wife Soraya, the two are covered in a veil and look upon each other through a mirror
  • Amir remarks that this is the first time he told her he loved her
  • Shortly thereafter Baba dies from cancer, his funeral has a wonderful turn out of Afghans who pay their respect for the man that helped them
  • Amir is amazed by the outpour and realize just quite how much Baba defined him
  • Following the funeral Amir spends more time getting to know Soraya's family and he learns that General Taheri is a very strict man, he even refuses to work and lives off of well fare because he feels work is below him
  • Amir also learns that when Soraya ran away he came and found her wielding a gun, he brought her home and forced her to cut off her hair as punishment
  • The novel fades to the summer of 1988 and Amir and Soraya are enjoying their marriage
  • Amir has written his first novel and the two plan on conceiving a child, however despite numerous attempts they cannot do so
  • They ponder adoption but General Taheri doesn't believe it would be a good idea
  • Soon thereafter Amir writes his second novel and the couple are able to move into a house in San Francisco, they continue to try to have a child yet cannot
  • Chapter 14
  • The novel then flashes forward to where it first began, June 2001
  • Amir has responded to Rahim Khan's phone call and request to travel to Pakistan
  • Amir decides to return and tells Soraya that he plans to go through with the visit as Rahim was the first adult Amir thought of as a friend and he is very ill
  • While Amir walks through Golden Gate Park and watches a father and son play catch he thinks about something Rahim said on the phone about Amir having a chance to be good again
  • Later that night while Amir lies in bed with Soraya he thinks about their relationship and how they're is no passion anymore as the two just seem to talk about work
  • While asleep he dreams of Hassan running through the snow
  • Soon after Amir leaves on his journey to Pakistan
  • Chapter 15
  • Amir arrives in the town of Peshawar, where Rahim currently lives
  • He takes a taxi to Rahim's home and the cab driver talks non stop on the state of Afghanistan
  • They drive through an area known as Afghan Town and it is a very dirty district with children selling all sorts of things on the streets
  • Amir thinks back to his last visit with Rahim the night before they left Afghanistan, he and Baba had kept in touch but Amir hadn't spoken to him since shortly after Baba's death
  • Eventually he arrives to see a sickly looking Rahim answer the door
  • The two talk over tea and Amir tells him of his marriage, career, and of Baba
  • Rahim shows him a scar over his eye and explains that he got it at a soccer match because the man next to him been to loud and obnoxious
  • Rahim also says that he had been living in Amir and Baba's home since they left because he wanted to take care of it as Baba planned to return
  • He explains that he lived there up until the fighting and war was too intense
  • Rahim tells Amir that he does not expect to live through the summer and that he called him to Pakistan because he wanted to see him one last time, as well as for another reason
  • Rahim explains that while he was living in Baba's home he had company, Hassan, and that he must tell Amir about him
  • Chapter 16
  • The novel shifts to 1986 with Rahim Kahn narrating, he explains that as he became to old to care for Baba's house on his own he set out to find Hassan for help
  • Hassan was living in a small hut in Hazarajat with his wife Farzana, a Hazara woman, who was pregnant
  • Rahim asks Hassan to return with his family to Baba's home to help take care of it, however initially Hassan refuses
  • That evening Rahim stays the night at the hut and he and Hassan discuss the past few years
  • Ali has died from a land mine
  • Hassan cries as he learns Baba has died and also asks many questions about Amir
  • The next morning Hassan awakes and changes his mind about Rahim's proposal and the family decides to move in with Rahim
  • Hassan returns to live in the servants quarters and spends the next year working to fix up the house
  • When Farzana gives birth the baby is stillborn and they bury the child in the yard
  • She again becomes pregnant in 1990, around that same time Hassan's mother, Sanaubar, appears at the gates of the house with cuts all over her face
  • Hassan and Farzana care for Sanaubar and help her recover, when it is time for Farzana to have her child Sanaubar helps deliver it as well as help take care of it
  • The child is named Sohrab from the stories Hassan and Amir read as boys
  • Sanaubar lives with the family for the next four years until she dies in 1995
  • During those years the soviets had been finally pushed out of Afghanistan however fighting was not over as rival Afghan groups still clashed
  • Also during the time Hassan has been working to teach Sohrab to read as well as fly kites
  • In 1996 the Taliban gain control over Afghanistan, Hassan's passion of kite flying is banned at that time
  • Chapter 17
  • The novel again shifts back to Amir's perspective as he speaks with Rahim
  • Amir asks about Hassan and where he currently lives
  • Rahim hands Amir an envelope from Hassan intended for Amir, in it is a picture of Hassan as well as a letter
  • The letter tells Amir how Kabul has changed since the Taliban have taken over
  • In the letter Hassan tells of how one day Farzana was struck by a man at the market because she spoke too loudly
  • Hassan finishes by saying that if Amir ever returns Hassan will be waiting for him
  • Rahim explains that Amir cannot see Hassan however as he was murdered by the Taliban after they found him in Baba's house
  • The Taliban would not believe he was watching it for someone and killed him mostly because he was Hazara
  • Farzana ran towards Hassan after he was shot only to be shot as well
  • Sohrab, Hassan's son, was taken to an orphanage after his parents were murdered
  • Rahim tells Amir that he must go rescue him, however Amir at first refuses
  • Rahim then adds that Baba often was afraid that Amir could never stand up for himself
  • Lastly Rahim shocks Amir with more details, Ali was never able to have children therefore Hassan was not his son, suggesting that Hassan was Baba's son
  • Amir is furious with Rahim after learning this and leaves the apartment
  • Chapter 18
  • Amir walks to a teahouse where he thinks about what he has just learned
  • He is so ashamed of himself and believes he caused his brothers death
  • Amir is also furious with Baba for keeping it a secret for all the years as Baba stole a brother from Amir, stole Hassan's identity, and stole Ali's honor
  • Amir realizes that Rahim's intentions are for Amir to atone for Baba's and his own sins
  • Amir decides to return to Rahim's apartment and tells him that he will rescue Sohrab, and in some sense rescue his dead brother
  • Chapter 19
  • Amir meets a man, Farid, who Rahim has arranged to take Amir to Kabul
  • Farid and his father resisted against the Soviet invasion, following the war Farid lost two daughters and part of his hand to a land mine
  • Once in Afghanistan Amir does not recognize the drastic changes that have occurred during the last twenty years
  • Farid asks Amir if he still considers Afghanistan to be his country, however before allowing an answer Farid says Amir was always a tourist in Afghanistan as he was sheltered by Baba's wealth
  • Farid then points to a poor man wandering the streets and remarks that he is the real Afghanistan
  • That evening Amir and Farid stop at Farid's brothers home to spend the night
  • The three have a conversation that evening over tea and Farid insults Amir by saying that he is only back to sell his land and return to America with his money
  • Amir states that he is actually returning to Afghanistan to rescue his illegitimate brother's son and bring him to safety
  • Farid's brother is moved by this and calls Amir a true Afghan
  • Farid and Amir eat dinner that night while the rest of the family in the house does not eat
  • Later before going to sleep Farid apologizes to Amir for insulting him and says he will help rescue Sohrab
  • In the middle of the night Amir is awoken by a nightmare where he sees Hassan being shot, Amir realizes that it was himself shooting Hassan in the dream
  • Amir decides to take a walk outside to relax and he overhears Wahid, Farid's brother, arguing with his wife
  • Amir learns that the family did not eat that night because they chose to feed Amir and Farid instead
  • Before leaving the next morning Amir leaves a large amount of cash under the mattress for the family
  • Chapter 20
  • Amir and Farid eventually make their way to Kabul and Amir realizes it is extremely different from what it once was
  • War has ravaged the land, buildings are destroyed, and beggars are everywhere
  • While in Kabul a Taliban patrol drives past Amir and Farid, Amir stares at the truck
  • Farid quickly scolds him saying that the Taliban will use any excuse for violence
  • Shortly after Amir meets a beggar who was once a literature professor
  • He learns that the beggar knew his mother Sofia, and Amir questions him about his mother before he has to continue on
  • Eventually the two find an orphanage where they believe Sohrab will be
  • At first they are declined entrance until Amir explains that he is Sohrab's uncle
  • Once inside they notice that there are hundreds of children and no where near enough supplies
  • The director of the orphanage informs them that Sohrab is no longer there, he explains that every month or so the Taliban come by and pay for a child or two
  • Amir is furious and threatens the director before controlling himself as he is in the presence of children
  • The director then says that he must accept the money in order to feed the other children, he then says that Sohrab was taken a month ago and that they might find him at Ghazi Stadium the next day
  • Chapter 21
  • Later that day the two travel to Baba's house and Amir walks around the estate shortly and reminisces before moving on to a hotel for the night
  • The next day the travel to Ghazi Stadium where a soccer match is being played
  • The pitch is entirely dirt and the crowd is very relaxed so as to not incite the Taliban
  • During half-time a man and woman are dragged onto the pitch and buried up to their wastes in dirt
  • A Taliban man announces to the crowd that they have committed adultery and shall be stoned to death
  • The two are quickly murdered and the second-half begins after their bodies are removed
  • Amir realizes that the Taliban man who has executed the man and woman is the man he needs to speak to regarding Sohrab
  • Amir speaks with other members of the Taliban who arrange the meeting
  • Chapter 22
  • Amir and Farid travel to the house where Amir will meet with the Taliban man about Sohrab
  • Amir enters a room where the Taliban official waits with multiple guards
  • One of the guards tears of Amir's fake beard before the official begins to discuss the show at the stadium where he killed the two adulterers
  • He then begins to brag about the door to door massacre of the Hazara people living in Afghanistan
  • The official then dismisses the guards and questions his intentions in Afghanistan
  • Amir explains he has come to find Sohrab
  • The official summons a guard and asks him to retrieve Sohrab who arrives in the room dressed in a blue silk outfit, mascara on his eyes, and bells around his ankles
  • The guard makes him do a dance before the official dismisses the guard again
  • The official then asks Amir what ever happened to Babalu, the name Assef used to call Ali
  • Amir is shocked as he realizes the official is in fact Assef
  • Amir immediately states that he will pay for the boys return, yet Assef says that money will not due
  • Assef explains that he is on a mission to rid Afghanistan of garbage, yet Amir believes it is more so ethnic cleansing, Amir demands Sohrab yet Assef will not give him up
  • Assef then pulls out brass knuckles and begins to finish where he left off decades ago
  • Amir is hit many times until he is miraculously saved by Sohrab
  • Sohrab pulls out a slingshot, shoots Assef in the eye, and the two escape outside where Farid is waiting in the car
  • Once in the car Amir passes out from his wounds
  • Chapter 23
  • Amir spends the next few days passing in and out of consciousness in a hospital in Peshawar
  • Amir has many broken bones in his face, ribs, and a ruptured spleen
  • Eventually he manages to wake up enough to speak with Farid and Sohrab who wait nearby
  • Farid gives Amir a note from Rahim Khan who has gone
  • The note says that Rahim was aware of everything that happened with Hassan
