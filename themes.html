Themes

  • Redemption
  • Amir spends nearly the whole novel seeking redemption
  • Early on he seeks redemption from his father, as he feels responsible for his mothers death, as she died giving birth to him
  • He believes that winning the kite tournament and handing his father the losing kite will bring about redemption, however this event seems to set the whole plot in motion
  • Later on Amir spends much of his time trying to redeem himself for the guilt he feels towards his best friend Hassan
  • Amir had learned early on that a child who does not stand up for himself becomes a man who cant stand up for anything
  • As a child he did not stand up for himself, and he spends his adult life trying to prove he has courage and stand up for what is right
  • The father-son relationship
  • Amir has a deep love and admiration for his father, however he often feels like his father does not return the favor
  • He is constantly trying to earn his father's love and respect, and at one point this even prevents him from attempting to stop Hassan's rape
  • Amir's father has trouble fully expressing his love for Amir, as he can not acknowledge that Hassan is also his son, and therefore feels guilty showing much love to Amir
  • When Hassan dies, Amir tries to fill the role of father to Hassan's son, however it does not seem quite right, as Sohrab, Hassan's son, has trouble accepting Amir, after the death of his parents and the abuse he has endured during his life
  • How political events affect one's life
  • Much of the novel and Amir's experiences are shaped by the troubled political situation in Afghanistan
  • As a young child Amir enjoys a happy life, and in Afghanistan the monarchy seems very under control
  • However as the Soviet's overrun the country, Amir sees struggle in his own life as well
  • Assef is given a position by the republic that allows him great power, which he uses against Amir as well as others
  • When Kabul, the Afghan capital is over run, Amir and his father are forced to flee to California
  • While back in Afghanistan the Taliban takes over the country, and Hassan is killed at this time
  • Hassan's death and Assef's power are both significant events in Amir's life
  • The effect the past has on a person
  • Many of the characters in the novel are in conflict with their past, however Amir and Sohrab seem to be the most affected by their past
  • Sohrab was so abused and mistreated in his past that any affection shown to him by Amir seems to scare him
  • He also has a very strong fear of abandonment, as both his parents died when he was younger, he even contemplates suicide at the thought that Amir might leave him
  • Amir is constantly struggling with his past, as he believes he is responsible for Hassan's death
  • He is constantly trying to redeem himself for his past mistakes, and he acknowledges that his past will always be with him
